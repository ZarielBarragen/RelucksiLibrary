What is Relucksi?

Relucksi is...

A card game set in a fictional universe I made up. Humans were granted magic in the form of a fast pased, live battle card game by who they assumed to be a Goddess, Relucksi.
She named the card game after herself.

How do you play?

- Each deck has a max of 24 cards
- You can only use a card once in battle unless the card is stated otherwise
- There will be card "Formats" (Ranged, Summoner, Trap, Etc.) and card "Types/Elements" (Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Etc.)
- Each person that participates in this "game" must wear a device on their wrist. This device holds your deck, allows you to draw cards, and it magically shuffles your deck.
This is to keep you on your toes during battles (and to stop cheating as you're not supposed to know what cards you'll be getting next).
You cannot remove the device no matter what.
- Cards are hidden around the world, but if one would like to make their own cards,
they can ask the higher being and she will appoint them as a "Cardsmith," someone who creates cards to sell.
- Since cards in this universe works like spells/magic, there will be no "spell" types
- This game isn't turn based, all attacks are done in real time
- Every player has a different amount of overall health points. Lowest possible being 1000, Highest being 1,000,000 and the average being 4000.
- You are allowed to draw up to 4 cards at the start of your turn, after that, it maxes out at 2 everytime you use at least 2 cards.
- To determine a winner, the fighters decide. But if no conditions are made, its when you run out of cards.
- While yes this is a card, magic, game, thing, theres no one stopping you from actually using your hands and feet to start fighting
- You can only hold a max of 4 cards in your hand, and have a max of 3 cards in your "Save for later" slot

What are card Formats?

Basically what the card in itself actually does. Here are what they are:
• Ranged: These cards allow for attacks from a distance, potentially hitting multiple targets or specific areas.
• Summoner: Summon creatures or entities to the battlefield with various abilities and stats.
• Trap: Activated under certain conditions, affecting opponents or altering the state of the game.
• Enhancer: Temporarily boost a player's abilities or strengthen other cards in play.
• Defender: Focus on protection and defense, blocking or reducing incoming damage.
• Manipulator: Alter the flow of the game by changing the rules, such as affecting turn order, reshuffling decks, or swapping cards.
• Bane: Specifically designed to counter or nullify certain card types or elements, adding a strategic layer to deck building.

What are card Elements?

You know what they are in a card game, dont be stupid. Here are the types of elements cards can have: - Fire: Focus on aggressive and powerful attack spells. Can cause lingering damage over time.
- Water: Specialize in adaptability and flow, with abilities to heal and cleanse.
- Earth: Emphasize strength and resilience, with a focus on defense and endurance.
- Air: Prioritize speed and evasion, with quick strikes and abilities to dodge attacks.
- Light: Associated with healing and protection, offering buffs and defensive barriers.
- Darkness: Deceptive and cunning, with spells to weaken opponents or hide one's own actions.
- Metal: Durable and unyielding, offering strong defenses and powerful, focused attacks.
- Nature: Connected to life and growth, with abilities to regenerate and summon creatures.
- Ether: Ethereal and mystical, with unpredictable effects and manipulation of game mechanics.
- Chaos: Wild and uncontrollable, with high-risk, high-reward spells that can affect all players unpredictably.
- Ice: Specializing in control and stasis, these cards could slow down opponents or freeze actions temporarily.
- Lightning: Focused on swift and high-energy attacks, often with chain effects that can strike multiple targets.
- Psychic: Involved in manipulation and mind control, potentially altering opponents' decisions or revealing their strategies.
- Time: Revolving around temporal manipulation, allowing for actions like delaying opponent moves or repeating past actions.
- Cosmic: Drawing on celestial power, these cards might involve unpredictable, large-scale effects.
- Toxic: Centered around decay and poison, these could weaken opponents over time or corrupt other elements.
- Mystic: Tied to ancient and arcane powers, offering complex but powerful spells that require careful planning.
